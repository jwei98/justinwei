About Me

Hello! My name is Justin Wei, and I'm a passionate learner and problem-solver. I currently attend Duke University and am studying Computer Science and Neuroscience. In my free time I enjoy being outdoors, spending time with friends, and in general challenging myself both mentally and physically. I'm extremely interested in advancing the unexplored frontiers of software engineering and neuroscience as well as the intersection of the two. You can find out more about what I'm involved in around campus below!


Activities

  1. Researcher at Nicolelis Lab- The Nicolelis Lab pioneers research regarding neuronal population coding, Brain Machine Interfaces, and neuroprosthetics. I think the research being done at this lab is fascinating as it lies at the intersection of both neuroscience and computer science, and has both academic and practical implications. I joined the lab in January 2017 and am currently working in the monkey lab.
  2. Delta Sigma Pi (DSP)- DSP is a professional business fraternity that brings together those interested in business, technology, and finance. I recently rushed and pledged the fraternity in the Fall of 2016, and as part of the pledge process, we were required to attend weekly meetings and classes to develop our professional selves. I've been impressed with and loved everyone I've met through DSP, and I've received incredible mentorship from upperclassmen in the fraternity. Because of the amount I've enjoyed being a part of the fraternity, I will serve as my chapter's Marketing Director during the Spring '17 rush period.
  3. Project Waves Staff & Crew Leader- Project Waves (PWaves) is a pre-orientation program for freshmen at Duke. About 80 freshmen have the opportunity to come to Duke a week before orientation to bond with their classmates at the Marine Lab and Outer Banks of North Carolina. The program is completely student-run, so as member of staff, I help plan this week for the incoming freshmen class in order to make them feel comfortable, bond with each other, and faciliate their trasntion to college. As a crew leader throughout the week, I serve as both a first mentor and friend to the incoming students.
  4. Duke Campus Representative- As a campus representative, I lead weekly tours for prospective students as well as serve as a panelist on info session panels. My role is to represent my university by providing these students with an honest, holistic view of Duke. Though I never thought I'd end up at Duke, after just one semester there's nothing I'd rather be than a Blue Devil, and I want to share my love for the school with future Dukies :).

Projects

  2. Auto Scheduler- This iOS app, which is currently in the making, is a collaboration between myself and two friends. The goal of the app is to help schedule events between large groups of people by accessing their calendars and recommending common available free times. My role in this project is mainly in developing the back-end software, which involves accessing the calendars and manipulating the information (done using arrays) to find common free times.
  3. Sonic Sharing- Sonic Sharing is a progressive web-app built during PennApps Hackathon XIV (Fall 2016). The app allows computers and devices to transfer information peer-to-peer without any network/bluetooth connection. Instead, this program uses sound emission/receving to share information.
